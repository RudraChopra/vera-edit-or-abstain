\begin{abstract}
We study a statistical deployment decision for representation interventions.
Given finitely many edits and certification evidence, the procedure must certify
one edit or abstain when deployment may reweight supported environment- and
source-conditional laws. VERA represents paired target harm and recovery by a
portfolio of retrained attackers as a vector of bounded contracts. It combines
candidate-wise intersection--union testing with simultaneous robust-risk curves
to report an anisotropic shift envelope, its limiting common radius, and every
unsupported coordinate. We prove fixed-profile and post-inspection validity,
derive exact curves for Bernoulli recovery and ternary paired harm, formulate a
convex minimax allocation for additive multi-cell contracts, and give an
exact-KL evidence lower bound for independent Bernoulli components with locally
matching margin and shift scaling. A
two-world theorem shows that required support absent from certification is not
certifiable without further assumptions. Learn Then Test supplies the finite-
family testing mechanism; the contribution is the intervention-specific
population object, evidence design, and support boundary. A prior IID study
found 35/128 violations for validation-only selection and 1/128 for fixed-
profile certification, but does not establish shift robustness.
\ControlledShiftAbstractResult
\end{abstract}

\section{A Statistical Decision Layer for Representation Edits}
\label{neurips:sec:intro}

Representation erasers construct mappings intended to suppress a concept while
retaining target-relevant information. Linear nullspace methods, adversarial
projections, closed-form erasure, nonlinear objectives, and manifold-aware
methods provide a rich intervention family
\cite{ravfogel2020null,ravfogel2022linear,belrose2023leace,ravfogel2022kernel,jourdan2023taco,holstege2025splince,avitan2026mance}.
Deployment introduces a different question. Which, if any, candidate remains
acceptable after accounting for certification uncertainty, target damage, probe
misspecification, and supported distribution shift?

Average validation performance is not an adequate contract. First, target
error after editing and target error before editing are paired on the same
example; their difference isolates incremental intervention harm. Second,
ordinary attacker accuracy changes with source prevalence and can hide high
conditional recall. Third, averaging environments or attackers can accept an
edit with one violated coordinate. Fourth, no confidence construction can
recover a required conditional absent from certification support.

VERA is a certifying decision layer, not a new eraser. It takes a finite family
of fitted interventions and registered downstream learners, tests one declared
profile using a candidate-wise intersection--union rule, and simultaneously
reports the set of supported profiles for which all contracts are established.
The set is a vector envelope; the largest diagonal point is a common-radius
summary. Empty evidence for a required coordinate sets that coordinate to zero
and forces abstention for profiles that use it.

We make four contributions. First, we define a supported conditional-
reweighting certificate coupling paired intervention harm and source-balanced
recovery by heterogeneous retrained attackers. Second, we establish finite-
sample validity for fixed profiles and simultaneous post-inspection envelopes,
then derive prospective one-cell and additive multi-cell evidence allocations.
Third, we give an indistinguishability theorem for unseen support. Fourth, we
conduct a preregistered controlled cross-modal comparison of five eraser families and
nine deployment rules, with seed-clustered efficacy, safety, usefulness,
geometry, allocation, and held-out-attacker endpoints.

\section{Prior Risk Control, Robust Evaluation, and Allocation}
\label{neurips:sec:related}

\paragraph{Finite-family risk control.}
Risk-controlling prediction sets and Learn Then Test (LTT) convert calibration
into tests over finite algorithm families
\cite{bates2021rcps,angelopoulos2025ltt}. Conformal Risk Control treats general
monotone losses, and Pareto Testing addresses multiple risks and objectives
\cite{angelopoulos2024crc,laufer2023pareto}. Prompt Risk Control applies related
machinery to LLM prompt selection and estimated covariate shift
\cite{zollo2024prompt}. Recent selective, joint, and group-conditional work
further couples risk, utility, acceptance, and group guarantees
\cite{xu2025scrc,yu2026joint,huang2026conditional}. Accordingly, VERA does not claim
novelty for finite-family testing, Bonferroni correction, multiple-risk
selective acceptance, or abstention alone.

\paragraph{Robust evaluation.}
Known importance weights and high-probability corrections address specified
covariate shifts \cite{tibshirani2019covariate,almeida2025hprc,zecchin2025wcrc}. Robust
validation and fine-grained robust conformal inference cover ambiguity sets
\cite{cauchois2024robust,ai2024finegrained}; CVaR concentration and robust model
evaluation provide closely related tools
\cite{thomas2019cvar,najafi2025federated,waudbysmith2024means}. Distributional
fairness certificates, worst-subpopulation effects, and robustness benchmarks
motivate conditioning rather than global pooling
\cite{kang2022certifying,ehyaei2025fragile,jeong2020worstcase,sagawa2020groupdro,koh2021wilds}.
Domain-adaptation impossibility and dataset-shift evaluation motivate making
support assumptions explicit rather than extrapolating them from validation
performance \cite{bendavid2010impossibility,subbaswamy2021evaluation}.
VERA specializes these ideas to a paired representation intervention, registers
several retrained attackers, and reports an entire supported profile set rather
than one robust scalar.

\paragraph{Erasure and evidence design.}
Probe dependence and behavioral counterfactual work motivate evaluating an
edit with more than its construction loss
\cite{hewitt2019control,pimentel2020probing,voita2020mdl,elazar2021amnesic}.
FARE and MMD-B-Fair certify restricted fairness objectives
\cite{jovanovic2023fare,deka2023mmdbfair}; VERA neither supersedes those
objectives nor proves universal guardedness. Residual nonlinear recovery and
fundamental utility limits further rule out reading eraser optimization as a
universal deployment guarantee \cite{akbari2025obliviator,chowdhury2025limits}.
Selective-classification systems reject individual examples
\cite{elyaniv2010foundations,geifman2017selective,geifman2019selectivenet}; VERA
instead rejects an intervention before deployment. Classical stratified allocation
motivates evidence targeting, but our formal claim is restricted to the frozen
multi-contract DKW power surrogate and registered stream architecture. We do
not claim a general allocation principle or global sample optimality; the
result is the exact optimizer of this certificate-specific surrogate under the
stated sampling architecture.

\section{Supported Conditional Reweighting Model}
\label{neurips:sec:model}

All probability statements condition on a sigma-field fixed before
certification: construction data, design data, fitted edits, target learners,
attacker portfolio, candidate family, thresholds, profiles, and tie-break rule.
Let $Z\in\mathbb R^d$ be frozen features, $Y$ a target, $S\in\{0,1\}$ a source
concept, $G$ an observed target environment, and $e\in\mathcal E$ an edit. The
identity representation is the paired comparator. If it is also selectable,
it must be included as a corrected candidate.

For certification observation $i$, paired target harm is
\begin{equation}
H_{e,i}=\mathbf 1\{f_e(Z_i^e)\ne Y_i\}
-\mathbf 1\{f_0(Z_i)\ne Y_i\}\in\{-1,0,1\}.
\label{neurips:eq:harm}
\end{equation}
For retrained attacker $a\in\mathcal A$, define Bernoulli correctness
$L_{e,a,i}=\mathbf 1\{a_e(Z_i^e)=S_i\}$. Construction and attacker training do
not use certification outcomes.

Given reference law $P$ and cap $\Gamma\ge1$, let
\begin{equation}
\mathcal Q_\Gamma(P)=\left\{Q\ll P:
0\le\frac{\mathrm dQ}{\mathrm dP}\le\Gamma\quad P\text{-a.s.}\right\},
\quad
\rho_{\Gamma,P}(V)=\sup_{Q\in\mathcal Q_\Gamma(P)}\mathbb E_Q[V].
\label{neurips:eq:ambiguity}
\end{equation}
The target vector contains
$\rho_{\gamma_g,P_g}(H_e)$ for every supported environment $g$. For attacker
$a$, leakage is
\begin{equation}
B_{e,a}(\eta_0,\eta_1)=\frac12\sum_{s\in\{0,1\}}
\rho_{\eta_s,P_s}(L_{e,a}).
\label{neurips:eq:leakage}
\end{equation}
The registered target and leakage thresholds are $\tau$ and $\lambda$.
Equal class weights make leakage invariant to source prevalence; environment-
conditional target contracts make arbitrary environment mixtures harmless once
each coordinate is controlled. These statements do not assume that target and
source conditionals form a single factorized law. Each registered conditional
must separately obey its cap.

The common population radius is the largest $\Gamma\ge1$ for which every target
and leakage contract holds on the diagonal profile. It is defined as zero when
the IID profile is not established. This radius measures sensitivity to a
declared class, not the magnitude of an unknown deployment shift.

\section{Fixed-Profile and Simultaneous Certificates}
\label{neurips:sec:certificate}

For bounded $V$, robust risk has the dual
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\Gamma,P}(V)=\inf_t\left\{t+
\Gamma\mathbb E_P[(V-t)_+]\right\}.
\label{neurips:eq:cvar}
\end{equation}
A Dvoretzky--Kiefer--Wolfowitz band yields one event that upper-bounds this
quantity for all $\Gamma\ge1$. The same event controls the continuum; no union
bound over uncountably many budgets is taken.

The discrete audit variables permit exact curves. If $L\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(p)$,
then $\rho_{\Gamma,P}(L)=\min(1,\Gamma p)$. If
$H\in\{-1,0,1\}$ has probabilities $(p_-,p_0,p_+)$ and
$a=\Gamma p_+$, $b=\Gamma(1-p_-)$, then
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\Gamma,P}(H)=
\begin{cases}
1,&a\ge1,\\
a,&a<1\le b,\\
a+b-1,&b<1.
\end{cases}
\label{neurips:eq:ternary}
\end{equation}
One-sided Clopper--Pearson bounds replace unknown probabilities. The harm
construction bounds $p_+$ above and $p_-$ below while preserving the simplex;
balanced leakage splits its component error across the two class recalls.

\paragraph{Fixed profile.}
At a declared profile, candidate $e$ is accepted only when every target and
attacker contract passes. Candidate-level error is $\delta/|\mathcal E|$. For
an unsafe candidate, choose one violated component. Accepting the candidate
implies undercoverage of that fixed component, so no component-wise union bound
is needed inside the IUT. A union bound over candidates controls selection.

\begin{theorem}[Fixed-profile intervention control]
\label{neurips:thm:fixed}
For a finite registered candidate family independent of certification, the
probability that the fixed-profile rule deploys any candidate violating at least
one registered target or balanced-leakage contract is at most $\delta$.
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Post-inspection envelope.}
Let the simultaneous target and source-class robust-risk curves be
$U^T_{e,g}$ and $U^L_{e,a,s}$. Define
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\mathcal E}_e=\left\{(\boldsymbol\gamma,\boldsymbol\eta):
U^T_{e,g}(\gamma_g)\le\tau\ \forall g,
\quad \frac12\sum_sU^L_{e,a,s}(\eta_s)\le\lambda\ \forall a\right\}.
\label{neurips:eq:envelope}
\end{equation}
The envelope uses simultaneous correction across all candidate--contract
curves because every curve is available after certification.

\begin{theorem}[Simultaneous supported envelope]
\label{neurips:thm:envelope}
With probability at least $1-\delta$, every profile in every reported
$\widehat{\mathcal E}_e$ satisfies all registered target and balanced-leakage
contracts. Environment mixture weights and source prevalence may be selected
after certification without another correction.
\end{theorem}

The proof event contains every candidate, contract, and curve. Linear mixtures
of bounded environment risks remain bounded, source prevalence is absent from
the standardized functional, and selection among already covered objects does
not spend probability again. The conclusion applies only to deployment laws in
the supported profile; it is not an unrestricted external-distribution claim.

\section{Evidence Requirements and Allocation}
\label{neurips:sec:allocation}

Suppose one bounded component has range $R$, robust cap $\Gamma_0$, and
population margin $m>0$ below its threshold. A sufficient DKW evidence size for
component power $1-\beta/J$ is
\begin{equation}
n\ge \frac{\Gamma_0^2R^2}{2m^2}
\left(\sqrt{\log\frac{2}{\alpha}}+
\sqrt{\log\frac{2J}{\beta}}\right)^2.
\label{neurips:eq:upper}
\end{equation}
This exposes the quadratic cost of shift cap and inverse margin within the
surrogate.

Many contracts are additive across evidence cells. Write
$R_j=\sum_c w_{jc}\rho_{jc}$ and collect the range, cap, confidence allocation,
and weight terms into nonnegative coefficients $b_{jc}$. For population margin
$m_j>0$, define the normalized allocation coefficients
$a_{jc}=b_{jc}/m_j$. A sufficient power condition is equivalently
\begin{equation}
\sum_c\frac{b_{jc}}{\sqrt{n_c}}\le m_j
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\sum_c\frac{a_{jc}}{\sqrt{n_c}}\le 1
\qquad\text{for every contract }j.
\label{neurips:eq:additive-power}
\end{equation}
For fixed total evidence and cell floors, the prospective minimax design is
\begin{equation}
\min_{\boldsymbol n,t}\ t
\quad\text{s.t.}\quad
\sum_c a_{jc}n_c^{-1/2}\le t\ \forall j,
\quad\sum_cn_c=N,
\quad n_c\ge n_c^{\min}.
\label{neurips:eq:convex}
\end{equation}
Because $n\mapsto n^{-1/2}$ is convex, this is a convex program. With one
additive contract and inactive floors, the KKT equations give
$n_c\propto a_{1c}^{2/3}$. With inactive floors, if each contract uses one
represented cell, shared-cell maxima reduce the design to squared scores.
Deterministic rounding preserves the total and floors and is compared with the
continuous objective.

An independent design fold estimates margins for its selected max-min-margin
candidate. The resulting allocation is fixed before certification, so
conditioning preserves validity for all candidates. The power target is only
for that design-selected surrogate. It is not a guarantee of equal power for
every edit.

\begin{theorem}[Exact-KL evidence lower bound]
\label{neurips:thm:lower}
For an all-safe Bernoulli product experiment and alternatives that make one
registered component unsafe, any procedure with type-I error at most $\alpha$
and power at least $1-\beta$ obeys an exact information lower bound obtained by
summing allocated samples times the corresponding Bernoulli KL divergences.
When safe and unsafe probabilities stay in a fixed compact subset of $(0,1)$,
this implies local total-evidence scaling
$\Omega(\log(1/(\alpha+\beta))\sum_j\Gamma_j^2/m_j^2)$.
\end{theorem}

The exact-KL form needs no interior restriction within the stated independent
Bernoulli product experiment. The quadratic margin expression does: near
parameter-space boundaries KL geometry changes. Likewise, the allocation
claim is limited to the registered DKW surrogate and separate streams for target
environments and source classes. A joint design that reuses one observation
across both contract families may admit a different optimum.

The inverse planning problem minimizes $\sum_cn_c$ subject to all additive
power constraints. It produces continuous and deterministic integer evidence
requirements, per-cell top-ups, solver gaps, and right-censoring at a registered
cap. Design-fold estimates are planning quantities; evaluation-oracle margins
are labeled retrospective benchmarks and cannot alter a confirmatory decision.

\section{Unsupported-Support Impossibility}
\label{neurips:sec:impossibility}

\begin{theorem}[No certification from absent support]
\label{neurips:thm:impossibility}
Suppose a required audit cell has zero probability under the certification law.
If the model class contains a safe world and an unsafe world that produce the
same protocol observations but disagree on the cell's contract, then any
certification-data-only protocol with uniform false-acceptance probability at most
$\delta$ accepts in the safe world with probability at most $\delta$ whenever
deployment may concentrate on that cell.
\end{theorem}

The two worlds induce the same distribution over every statistic available to
the protocol and hence the same randomized acceptance probability. Controlling
false acceptance in the unsafe world bounds acceptance in the safe one. This is
an identification failure rather than a wide-interval problem. It applies to an
unseen target environment and to a missing source class needed for balanced
leakage. New supported observations or an explicit transport assumption are
required to move the boundary.

Camelyon17 provides a concrete instantiation. Certification observes hospitals
0, 1, 3, and 4, while deployment hospital 2 is absent; its relevant binary
source slice is single-class. VERA must abstain on a five-hospital statement.
This does not claim that hospital 2 violates the target contract or that the
dataset establishes clinical safety.

\section{Controlled Experimental Design}
\label{neurips:sec:protocol}

The prospective matrix uses seeds 45--108, four supported datasets, and five
official upstream eraser families: INLP, R-LACE, LEACE, TaCo, and MANCE++.
It contains 1,280 dataset--method runs and 3,072 candidate-edit evaluations.
Waterbirds supplies vision, CivilComments and Bios supply text, and GaitPDB
supplies time series. Camelyon17 is excluded from supported-shift endpoints and
used only for the support boundary
\cite{koh2021wilds,sagawa2020groupdro,borkan2019civilcomments,dearteaga2019bios}.

The 12 candidates per dataset--seed block are INLP ranks
$\{1,2,4,8\}$, R-LACE ranks $\{1,4\}$, one LEACE map, TaCo removal counts
$\{1,2,3,5\}$, and one MANCE++ configuration. Pinned upstream entry points run
under a common frozen-feature protocol. This is entry-point parity, not
reproduction of every upstream paper's full pipeline or eraser state of the
art; no proxy implementation contributes a candidate or result. Each edit
retrains a logistic target learner plus logistic, RBF--Nystroem
logistic, random-forest, and two-layer-MLP
attackers. A gradient-boosted tree is fitted on the training partition but
withheld from edit construction, selection, and certification.

The official training split is divided into eraser-training and construction
partitions. Pinned edit adapters use them as required, while fresh target and
attacker models fit only the eraser-training partition. Design, certification,
and evaluation streams then have distinct roles. A design fold chooses a feasible rare supported cell and creates an exact
reweighting of the finite reference law at the requested density-ratio cap. It
also fixes evidence allocation before certification. Independent certification
draws determine bounds. Exact expectations under the finite shifted law determine primary
contract violations; a 50,000-draw stream is an independent replay. Receipt
fields include support counts, probability-vector and profile hashes, split
hashes, upstream revisions, and per-example audit-array hashes. This design
establishes ambiguity membership for the controlled finite law but does not
generate new deployment subjects.

The primary profile requests global cap $\Gamma=1.1$ and uses its exact induced
conditional profile, with $\delta=0.05$ and 4,000 observations per
dataset--seed block. The fixed $(\tau,\lambda)$ values are $(0.40,0.70)$ for
Bios, $(0.075,0.80)$ for CivilComments, $(0.20,0.55)$ for GaitPDB, and
$(0.10,0.90)$ for Waterbirds. They were selected deterministically on earlier
design-only seeds and are benchmark operating points, not normative clinical or
legal tolerances. The targeted primary gives every evidence cell at least 15\%
of the budget; uniform allocation is matched. Secondary budgets are
$\{1000,2000,4000,8000\}$ and requested global caps are
$\{1.1,1.25,1.5\}$. Common radii are
right-censored at $\Gamma_{\max}=8$.
The preregistration sets the inversion cap to 8, whereas an earlier analysis
implementation used cap 4. Receipt generation and fixed-profile certification
are unaffected. We treat cap 8 as the prespecified analysis, retain the
unchanged cap-4 output as a sensitivity, require agreement with an independent
cap-8 replay, and report every resulting decision or gate difference.

The 64-seed sample size was fixed from the disjoint seeds 13--44 design block.
Its 14 favorable, zero adverse, and 18 tied seed-level differences gave
Jeffreys-smoothed exact-sign-test power above 0.999 at 64 seeds. Sixty-four also
provides 16 rotating sentinel decisions per dataset and exceeds the exact
59-seed minimum needed for a zero-event one-sided 95\% upper bound of 0.05.

Nine rules use identical candidates and evidence: always deploy, validation
point selection, IID LTT, robust point selection, generic scalar robust
certification, VERA fixed-profile certification, vector-envelope eligibility,
common-radius eligibility, and an exact shifted-law opportunity oracle. The scalar rule
averages threshold-normalized robust upper bounds; the vector rule requires all
coordinates. Utility tie-breaking is fixed.

A predefined supporting stress recomputes always deploy, validation point
selection, and the vector envelope at
$\kappa\in\{0.75,1,1.25\}$, with
$\tau_d(\kappa)=\kappa\tau_d$ and
$\lambda_d(\kappa)=0.5+\kappa(\lambda_d-0.5)$. The complete 36-cell grid is
reported. This analysis cannot alter a primary gate; its supporting condition
requires each naive rule to reach 20\% violations somewhere at the registered
contract, VERA to remain at or below 5\% in every dataset--severity cell, and
the registered safety and usefulness gates to pass.

Inference treats each seed's full four-dataset block as one independent unit.
The efficacy endpoint is a two-sided exact sign test on within-seed violation-
count differences between point and vector rules. The rotating-dataset safety
sentinel uses one prespecified decision per seed and a one-sided exact 95\%
Clopper--Pearson upper bound; any VERA false acceptance fails. Useful retention
requires a 95\% percentile-bootstrap lower bound of 20\% from 20,000 whole-seed
resamples with fixed seed 2027071601, and vector retention must be at least twice
common-radius retention. The claim passes only if every gate passes.
Per-dataset values are secondary with Holm correction.

\section{Limitations and Responsible Use}
\label{neurips:sec:limits}

The guarantees are finite-reference and conditional on registered candidates,
target learners, attackers, thresholds, support, and shift caps. The caps are
sensitivity parameters, not estimates of an unknown deployment. Finite
attackers cannot establish information-theoretic erasure. Target control does
not extend to every downstream task. Algorithmic seeds quantify fitting and
split variability but do not sample new populations. Separate target and source
streams restrict allocation optimality, and controlled reweighting establishes
membership only for the finite replay law. Camelyon17 is an impossibility
